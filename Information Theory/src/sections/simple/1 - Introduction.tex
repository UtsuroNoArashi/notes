    At the core of modern computer science, among others, 
        two fields have become fundamental: \emph{information theory} 
        and \emph{data compression}. The former deals on how 
        informations can be transmitted through a channel; 
        the latter with methods to maximize the throughput of the channel.
        More correctly, data compression studies how to 
        efficiently encode data; that is, how to store informations efficiently.

    In what follows, we discuss topics of both fields in parallel; 
        we choosed this approach as the two fields are closely related.
